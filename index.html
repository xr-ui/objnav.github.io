CRG-Nav: Confidence-Aware Reasoning with Geometric Verification for Exploration and Zero-Shot Object Navigation

Shoubin Chen1, Rui Xu1, Zehao Wu1 Kai Zhang1†
1Guangdong Laboratory of Artificial Intelligence and Digital Economy (SZ); Shenzhen University
†Corresponding author
Paper Code arXiv

Abstract

Zero-shot object-goal navigation requires an agent to explore unknown environments and navigate to a target object, which remains a major challenge in embodied intelligence. Although foundation-model-based zero-shot object navigation methods alleviate the high training cost and poor generalization of traditional training-based approaches, existing methods still suffer from unreliable long-range perception, hallucination-induced decision bias, and inaccurate stopping decisions. To address this issues, we propose CRG-Nav, a modular zero-shot object navigation framework that decouples perception from decision-making for more reliable navigation. Specifically, CRG-Nav combines a vision-language model(VLM) with YOLO-World for open-vocabulary perception and employs a large language model(LLM) to evaluate candidate directions under a unified scoring criterion. In addition, we introduce a Gaussian-decay-based confidence mechanism to downweight unreliable long-distance observations, thereby constructing a more robust global exploration preference map. For more accurate stopping, CRG-Nav adopts a dual semantic-geometric verification mechanism to estimate the distance between the agent and the target object and determine whether termination is appropriate, effectively reducing false stops and premature stopping. We evaluate CRG-Nav on the HM3Dv1, HM3Dv2, and MP3D datasets. Compared with other state-of-the-art methods, CRG-Nav achieves excellent performance with only a small number of parameters and without task-specific training. Furthermore, real-world experiments demonstrate its effectiveness in physical environments.

Method Overview

An overview of our method. The agent first surveys its current location and selects observations from 6 angles, stitching them together to form a panoramic image. Then, the agent evaluates each angle and chooses the one with the highest preference score for subsequent exploration. If the target is detected during the exploration process, the verification module is activated to verify precise arrival at the target.
